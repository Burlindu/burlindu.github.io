The 30 DEG Rule

------------------------------------

"The sun will be low in the sky (say 30 deg) at breakfast and dinner in mid summer - but will be that same height in the sky (30 deg) at noon in mid winter."

"Therefore noon light in mid winter is what we think of as 'marginal sun' in mid summer"

------------------------------------

The Sun is at 30 deg in the sky:

- early morning and late evening in mid-summer
- late morning and early evening in the shoulder seasons
- at noon in mid winter

------------------------------------

Note of course that at noon the sun is direct to the south (more or less), and that early and late sun are way off to the east and the west. So the angle change occurs not only in height but also in direction. However isolating the angle change regardless of direction emphasizes the strong effect of earth axis tilt bewteen seasons.
