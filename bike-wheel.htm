The issue with wheels is size

Popular 622 ETRTO / 29" / 700 wheels are big and don't leave room to have at the same time:
- Thick city tires (say 1.5" 40mm+ ish)
- Fenders (even just 5mm)
- Toe Clearance
- big wheels fight a compact frame geometery

SOLUTION

Using a 584 650B wheel
instead of a 622 29" wheel

Tires add diameter between 50mm (1" tire) to say 80mm (1.5") = 30mm added
So we're trying to get that 30mm back
622 - 584 = 38mm
That's it.
So you are getting 2 things:
(1) the extra 30mm to go for a 1/2" bigger tire
(2) Another 8mm for fenders and stuff

*** Crucially the outer rolling diameter of the wheel assembly (Tire+Wheel) stays the same or smaller - making toe clearance same or better, and enabling tidy frame design.

Bigger wheels force the head tube up, raising the top tube and make the whole downtube area harded to fit how you want (ie as seen in so many MTB downhill bikes with curves and structural plates on the downtube / headtube area)
