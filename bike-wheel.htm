The issue with wheels is size

Popular 622 ETRTO / 29" / 700 wheels are big and don't leave room to have at the same time:
- Thick city tires (say 1.5" 40mm+ ish)
- Fenders (even just 5mm)
- Toe Clearance (Glider has 1.75" to fender; want 2"+)
- big wheels fight a compact frame geometery
(ie Forcing up the Top Tube is bad)

SOLUTION

Using a 584 (650B / 27.5) wheel
instead of a 622 (700 / 29er) wheel
(my wheel is 590 but is old so tire selection is limited)

Tires add diameter between 50mm (1" tire) to say 80mm (1.5") = 30mm added
So we're trying to get that 30mm back
622 - 584 = 38mm
That's it.
So you are getting 2 things:
(1) the extra 30mm to go for a 1/2" bigger tire
(2) Another 8mm for fenders and stuff

*** note a thicker tire on a smaller wheel can give around the same rolling diameter.
I am less concerned with the gain or loss of efficency of a slightly bigger or smaller rolling diameter and more concerned with toe clearance, fender clearance & forcing the top tube higher
