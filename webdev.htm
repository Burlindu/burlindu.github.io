So the methodology is:

(1) Load up in CHROME both C:/Users/Burl/Documents/web/2024%20Surf3/burlindu.github.io and https://burlindu.github.io/

(3) Load up the GITHUB DESKTOP and hit the Blue-ish buttons (there's a couple of hits, first bottom L 'COMMIT TO MAIN' and then a confirmation of some sort on the R, 'PUSH ORIGIN') to sync the HOSTED files with the LOCAL files. Github.io now matches the new LOCAL code, or it should

(4) DOUBLE CHECK the upload is ok with local and web CHROME views the same - HOSTED web propogation will take a minute

(5) If impatient you can load the GITHUB WEB interface and check that your github.io now matches the new LOCAL code

TRIANGLIFY
SURF II uses an abstract geometric background that is 'Tessilated'.
In the first year of my M.Arc we had a project that asked for a 'tessilated' landscape & I made one out of triangles (made of paper) all modified in their geometries to make a 3d landscape out of an endless series of flat surfaces (mine was called 'between house'). It looked cool. I wanted a digital version - assemetrical triangles all fitting together in a field, differentiated by a natural colour gardiant (as if the light were hitting all the triangles at different angles - ie as if it we a flat image of an undulating, triangulated 3d surface). I figured some nerd on the internet might have what I was looking for. That nerd is Quinn Rohlf. His TRINGLIFY app (which was a high-school project he just posted on the web) was exactly what I was looking for in every way and his near-default image became my webpage background.
Later I was watching my favorite new show THE ORVILLE and saw that the TV producers had used TRINGLIFY to generate art for the starship. I Twitter'd Quinn R to let him know.
