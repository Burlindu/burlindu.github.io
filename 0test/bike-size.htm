Additional Notes

Note also the SM is a road handlebar
And the XL is a City handlebar
So the ROAD layout has the grip LOWER and AHEAD of the red line - the meet of head and top tubes
The CITY layout has the grip waaay back and higher -
so here the 'SMALLER' frame gets lengthened and the "XL' frame gets shorter.
Like way shorter. It's like they are both trying to be 'medium' bikes.

So (DUH! - sorry Rivendell) the number we need in addition to that frame length is around what our seat to grip length would be................

So I added Y AXIS -
Y is the same in both images.
For the SM frame it is seat-yo-grip (ROAD BARS)
For the XL frame it is seat-to....beyond grip (CITY BARS)
CONCLUSION: The actual reach (seat-to-grip) on the SM road bike is longer than on the XL City bike. If sizing seems dumb/hard using the seat tube length, it is also iffy when using the top tube length, given tgat a higher seat is furthur back and and city bar may be way behind a road bar.

WE NEED TO SEPARATE BIKE FRAME HEIGHT AND BIKE FRAME LENGTH BECAUSE THE SAME HEIGHT RIDER MAY WANT A TOTALLY DIFFERENT BIKE LENGTH DEPENDING ON RIDE STYLE (RACE OR COMFORT) - AND THE LISTINGS SHOW 'SIZE' BY BASICALLY SEAT TUBE LENGTH WHICH IS TOTALLY IRRELEVANT FOR THIS (OR MOSTLY IRRELEVANT)
