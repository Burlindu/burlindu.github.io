MORE DESIGN NOTES


It sounds like MTB use short rear stays for better steep climbing (that's what Joe Breeze said - he also noted aim for MTB of having top down tube meet as far away as possible on head tube, for strength)
This might help explain why Rivendell goes the other way with a long back triangle - ie NOT for steep dirt climbs
I know I like a slack front & back end...... (and short upright ride)

So I'm looking at:
- Slack head and seat tube, even 70 deg
- level top tube
- short top tube, like 550 and underline
- lowest possible top tube
- assume med or long rear tringle - softer ride & better tire clear (and likely better for panniers too)
- smallish wheels (say 650b) for better tire clear, shorter wheelbase, and easier overall frame design - and better toe clear too
