The 30-Degree rule is my own 'rule' showing how much the sun's angle in the sky varies over seasons. It's always at 30 degrees in the sky (pretty low) sometime - but in mid summer that's in the morning and in the evening, while in mid winter that's as high as it gets at noon.

This means that in mid-winter when the sun is still below the horizon - it's dark out - by that same time in mid summer the sun is as high in the sky as it gets in noon in mid-winter. Mid-winter high noon sun occurs in summer, defacto, before sunrise and after sunset.
