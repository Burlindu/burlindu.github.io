Red Peugeot Road Bike

Vintage 1970's - White bike below is 71 or Green catalogue 1972 looks like it
Amazing paint & textured rims
Frame pump mounts
Center pull MAFAC brakes
I removed front derailer & added ORIGIN 8 track seat
A fragile but beautiful bike
Purchase about $150 from bike shop in Kensington
Could have used: upgreade in pulls, shifters, cables & bads
Replaced rear derailler (orig one exploded during a ride) (just a base TOURNEY)
I put on lovely CONTINENTAL ULTRASPORT tires 32-630 / 27x1.25



One of the reasons I wanted the vintage bike is it had/has brake extension pulls at the grips - I hardly ever ride down in Aero position.
Shifters also located on stem instead of down on frame. However shifters not on the grips or brakes is a pain

New grips were needed, tried a few kinds (wanted old sleek/slim vinyl/poly, settles on a cotton wrap), seatpost (orig was a stub) & seat (orig was dried out leather and was, in any case, stolen off the bike while parked).


Internet says this is a 1975 Chrolux rim, like mine.
1970's saw influx of cheap mass produced bike. Mine may be that - looks like 70's lineup had the PCX10 at the top & the UO8 as the non-race most popular ver - that looks to be it.

*** mid 70's bike not leather seats - but looks like early 1970's UO8's did.

Wiki - "The most popular and best known of Peugeot's performance bicycles was the PX-10E, one of the most affordable professional racing bikes in the 1960's and 70's"

from FB - "brazed on pump pegs, and the stamped rear dropouts are tell tale signs of a UO8"

Wiki - "The Peugeot UO 8 was a ten-speed bicycle manufactured by Cycles Peugeot during the "bike boom" of the 1970s. It was marketed as a mid-range "racing style" cycle, and was manufactured from sometime in the early 60s[1] until the end of 1980.[2] The model was discontinued for the 1981 model yea"



This is a 1977 UO8 and looks like mine - note my close-up photo also shows a frame mount for shifters, so I'm not certain that the stem mount is orig (but heck it sure looked orig & matched the brake extensions)

1972 UO8 on FB has pump mounts. Note my cheap plastic badge - for sure was not a PX-10, the proper racer.

from Reddit - "The AO8 does indeed have the chromed fork ends. The UO8 was a step-down in components compared to the AO8- the frame was pretty much the same IIRC"
** I think this is wrong - other notes say the AO8 was lower end with cheaper components, UO8 slightly higher **



*** This white 1971 UO8 looks dead on (Rims and pump mounts) but has frame shifters. So maybe mine was more 1971 vintage and someone later added teh stem shifter mount from say a 77. The orig Simplex Deraillers are said to fail big time - mine sure did.

SOLD circa 2015 - to a hipster dude with a touque perched on his head. I gather from his reaction he was thrilled with the cool looking bike. A perfect fit for the next owner - I had it on the wall, he I think was going to ride it, and he had all the desired modern-hipster retro style. I sold for $150 - same as my purchase price, so teh newer tape, seat, post and labour get thrown in for goodwill. Selling at purchase price nmeans ownership cost parts + inflation - that's a fine deal. It looked out-of-date when I got it, my simple mods really let the great graphics/details and geometry shine.

I loved the Red Peugot however the thin rims and rickety brakes and shifting - and the super narrow leverage on the road bars - just felt a bit unsafe for my hardy city rising. Was a great hanging display bike. My switch to the used Champange Miele was the right move, a perfect vintage city utility bike.
