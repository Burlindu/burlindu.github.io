ChromeCast Audio / Google Home Nest (+ Tivoli)
Got one of the last Chromecast Audio after they were discontinued
Hooked up in conjunction with first a Google Home puck (voice control - Youtube Music streaming) and then with the Google Nest Home screen/voice controller - a wonderful kitchen system.

The hookup is:
Chromcast Audio puck miniplug wires into AUX port on Tivoli Model 1
Google Puck or Nest Screen set (via software) so that Chromecast Audio puck is default Audio device
Now voice commands at Puck or Nest Screen will feed through the Chromcast Audio to the Tivoli AUX and play on the Tivoli, not the Puck or Nest. Voila!

It's a compact kitchen system heavy on interface (screen & voice) and light on drivers (3"ish full range) but the Tivoli is punch and bottom-ported and sounds great as near field fun sound (voice or music)
Xtra
Ikea Eneby 20 Bluetooth (& AUX minijack) extra speaker
Xmas gift from John (I asked for it)

Theory was that this inexpensive speaker would be good based on 3 things:
(1) Wood box (thin MDF, but still)
(2) 2 Drivers and a rear port (sort of a real speaker)
(3) Most importantly, big volume knob
(3a) (designed in conjunction with teenage engineering)

I love the simple Eneby. It sounds good enough. The volume knob is great. The AUX port is a fail as you still have to hold-press the volume knob and it just gets confused as to input, so I mostly just use as bluetooth. It was on sale for something like $40 - an amazing value. And plug-in - you can buy a battery pack, but I wanted a desktop plug-in speaker, not an always-portable.
Also the little JBL is a very mediocre sounding portable BT speaker that won a red dot because it has a great boxy shape
Ikea hack of the Hemnes TV stand - now 1 center drawer so the A5's fit in the cabinet, with the back cut at the side for audio porting. It looks clean and sounds good if the unit is off the wall (Hemnes has wheels added to move out for best viewing anyway)
