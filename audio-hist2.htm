The Ten

In the 1980's Uncle Julian gave me ten or so vinyl albums

They are the bedrock upon which my music collection (now just playlists and streaming tagged albms) is founded.

Sure, I bought Bowie's Let's Dance (my first vinyl) and Howard Jones, etc on my own between say grade 8 and 11. But at some point - I'm guessing grade 11 or 12, in my big double room at Appleby with the IKEA cupboatd turntable stand (with the Realistic Lab 1500 turntable and Panasonic RX C39 Boom Box), Uncle Julian gave me a small collection of vinyl different than what I had heard before. I didn't like all the albums - but Blood on the Tracks was there, and Desire, and that started it all.
The vinyl was old - heavy discs and thick textured sleeves. Sticky Fingers had the real working zipper. They were beautiful.
